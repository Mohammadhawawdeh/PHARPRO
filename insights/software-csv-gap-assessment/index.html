Most pharmaceutical companies operating across MENA have at least 10–30 GxP-critical computerised systems. LIMS, ERP, SCADA, MES, EDMS, quality management platforms, validated spreadsheets — each one potentially carrying a compliance gap that regulators can and do cite during inspections.

The question is not whether gaps exist. The question is whether you find them before the inspector does. PHARPRO's Software & CSV Gap Assessment is a structured, expert-led review of your validated system landscape — pinpointing compliance gaps in critical areas such as electronic signatures, data integrity, and system validation — mapped against FDA 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP Annex 11, and GAMP 5, and delivered with six concrete outputs including a written gap report, risk assessment, CAPA plan, and compliance roadmap.

Inspectors work from structured checklists. They know exactly which 21 CFR Part 11 and Annex 11 requirements to probe. If your documentation doesn't answer those probes before they walk in, you are already behind. A gap assessment changes that.

GxP-critical spreadsheets identified, validation status confirmed, and gaps against 21 CFR Part 11 and data integrity expectations documented.

The Most Common Gaps PHARPRO Finds

Critical Missing or incomplete IQ/OQ/PQ documentation for GxP-critical systems — no approved protocols, no test evidence, no conclusion report
Critical Audit trail not enabled, or enabled but not reviewed — one of the most cited 21 CFR Part 11 and Annex 11 failures
Critical GxP-critical spreadsheets in active use with no validation package — no URS, no testing, no version control
Major System categorised incorrectly under GAMP 5 — Category 1 treatment applied to Category 4 or 5 systems
Major Validation documentation not updated following system upgrades, patches, or configuration changes — change control gap
Major No Validation Master Plan (VMP) covering the complete computerised system landscape
Minor Periodic review overdue — no evidence of annual system review as required by Annex 11 and good practice
Minor Missing traceability — URS requirements not linked to test cases; RTM absent or incomplete

The Assessment Methodology — 6 Steps

1
Step 1
System Evaluation
A detailed review of your current computerised systems to identify compliance gaps across key regulatory standards — US FDA 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP Annex 11, and GAMP 5. All GxP-critical systems are included: LIMS, ERP, SCADA, MES, EDMS, QMS, laboratory instruments, and validated spreadsheets.
📋 System register with regulatory obligations mapped
2
Step 2
GAP Analysis
A thorough GAP analysis comparing your current system capabilities against required regulatory requirements, with focus on critical areas: electronic signatures, data integrity controls, and system validation documentation (URS, IQ/OQ/PQ, RTM, VSR).
📊 Gap matrix — system by system, requirement by requirement
3
Step 3
Risk-Based Assessment
Evaluation of the risks associated with non-compliance — identifying areas of vulnerability that may impact product quality, patient safety, and data integrity. Every gap is classified by severity (critical / major / minor) with supporting evidence references.
⚠️ Risk-classified finding register
4
Step 4
Recommendations & CAPA
Actionable recommendations to mitigate identified risks, ensuring all systems align with industry regulations. A Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) plan is included to guide system improvements with clear ownership and target timelines.
📋 CAPA plan with prioritised remediation actions
5
Step 5
Regulatory Alignment
Ensuring all system features and processes align with regulatory requirements — including documentation, change control, and the validation lifecycle. A regulatory alignment checklist is produced for each system against 21 CFR Part 11, Annex 11, and GAMP 5.
✅ Regulatory alignment checklist per system
6
Step 6
Documentation & Reporting
A comprehensive written assessment report is delivered — detailed findings, actionable recommendations, and a strategic compliance roadmap outlining every step needed to close gaps and achieve regulatory alignment. A review meeting is held to walk through all findings and answer your team's questions.
📄 Written Gap Report + Compliance Roadmap + review meeting

Optional next step: PHARPRO can execute the remediation — writing validation documentation, executing qualification protocols, or implementing corrective actions identified in the gap report. This is available as a separate engagement based on your priorities and timeline.

Common Questions

How is this different from an internal self-assessment?

Internal assessments are valuable but carry blind spots — teams often validate against their current understanding of requirements, which may not reflect the latest regulatory guidance or inspector expectations. PHARPRO brings external expertise, current regulatory knowledge, and an inspector's perspective that internal teams rarely replicate.

What if my systems are partially validated?

Partial validation is the most common finding — and exactly what the gap assessment is designed to address. PHARPRO maps what exists, documents what's missing, and prioritises remediation by regulatory risk. You don't need to be fully validated to start an assessment; the assessment tells you where you are.

Do you work remotely or on-site?

Both. Documentation reviews are conducted remotely via secure file sharing. System configuration reviews may be conducted remotely (screen share/VPN access) or on-site depending on system type and client preference. PHARPRO serves clients across Jordan, UAE, KSA, Egypt, and internationally.
